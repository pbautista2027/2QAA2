COLLECTION

Embroidered in our Paths

Description

This collection is composed of three Petrarchan sonnets and three Shakespearean sonnets. It is about the people we love and admire. It allows the reader see how we view these people and how we express our love for them. We want to show that even if love is one feeling that the contrast between our works and how we experience love is what makes love so delightful

The most captivating star Composed by: Tanya Annika Jimenez

The world stopped spinning
I could see music and hear all of life’s color
It was always sunny, but only now did it feel like summer
I felt new, like my life was just beginning
Ever since I met you, my heart has been grinning
Your presence alone is what I need, no other
For you, I will be as constant as the flow of a river
In a world where I always lose, it finally felt like I was winning

Even if I am out of sight, I will be as loyal as the stars are to the sun
I will always be here to assure you that it will be just fine
Only with you, shall I ever run
Even if you rust, to me you always shine
The sun is still a star, yet it is still the brightest one
And you are just a person, however only you are mine

Fell Composed by: Paolo Eli Bautista

What if I told you that I've fallen?
A heart-shaped arrow through my chest it bore,
In morning's light, your breakfast I'll adore,
And when you're lost, I'll be your beckoned calling,

Yet ceaseless, this affection will enthrall,
I'll answer whether welcome or ignored,
A captive of the image I adored,
Upon my phone, a love that won't appall.

Don't say my name, extinguish this hot flame,
I'll grasp this feeling, though you disbelieve,
A fall, a fire, a longing to retrieve.

Yet, even in descent, I bear no shame,
For in the plunge, a strength is subtly known,
I'll rise alone, with heart and soul resewn.

Shakespearean Sonnet

Though shall enter my heart’s abode Composed by: Paolo Eli Bautista

In love's embrace, I thee profess my truth,
Yet silent, my affection hides its proof.
A call, my love, I'd gladly make for thee,
But only if thou wishest it to be.

Oh, let not love's sweet current find its end,
I pledge, my dearest, it shall not suspend.
No cease to love, my dearest, shall I find,
Yet hearken, love, to words that fill my mind.

Within mine heart, a sanctuary wide,
Inviting thee, my love, to stay inside.
Seek answers there, where stars above may gleam,
Speed on, my love, through life's celestial stream.

Whate'er fate brings, my dearest one, forgive,
For I am sorry, let our love outlive.
No other path my footsteps e'er could trace,
In love's sweet journey, thou art my sole grace.
