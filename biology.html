Biology

Our students have conducted multiple activities in their Biology subject in our Laboratory on the campus. From learning about cells to learning about applying biology in designing solutions to problems, we have the facilities to impart knowledge to the students. The following are pictures and outputs of Grade 9 students in their Biology 3 subject.



The digestive system in a human being has four main stages: ingestion, digestion, absorption, and elimination. In this project, we made use of soda crackers, a banana, water, citrus juice, vegetable oil, plastic cups, an old sock and a tray to demonstrate the process of food digestion in humans.





Biology Laboratory

Section 1: Stages of Food Processing

Crushing Stage

Putting bananas and crackers in the zip lock signifies ingestion. The addition of water represents salivary amylase being mixed with food. The zip-locked bag represents the stomach. Crushing the food simulates the strong muscle contractions on-going in the stomach when food is being digested. We were showing the addition of pepsin to the bolus of food by adding orange juice because they both have high pH levels which makes them acidic.







Transfer Stage

The liquid flowing out of the stocking shows osmosis, the absorption of water, in the intestine. The tray represents the lumen of the intestine. The material left in the stocking represents the feces. Pushing the partially solidified content from the old sock shows elimination.







Section 2: Simulation of Absorption


The paper towel that absorbed the most water is the four-folded paper towel. The layers create air pockets which causes the water to be absorbed by the paper towels. The organ that may be compared to the paper towel is the small intestine. The villi lines the small intestine, and it multiplies the surface area available for nutrient absorption and microvilli are on each epicell on the villi.







Section 3: Simulation of Bile Action


The bile action in the small intestine is exhibited by the dishwashing liquid causing the vegetable oil and water to mix B. This is similar to bile being a way of mechanical digestion as this is just a way to cut down triglycerides instead of chemically breaking it down.









Conclusion

We learned that food mixes with different enzymes to be broken down further and further until it can go through osmosis and turn into feces which will later be excreted. Another thing we learned from this is that the folds in the intestine cause greater water absorption, and bile is a powerful enzyme The simulation demonstrated in this activity was to show us a glimpse on the human digestive process and how we may be aware of digestive disorders that may affect us, and problems that may occur in our digestive system.
